Wines from Oregon

Pinot Noir

Pinot Noir is a red grape that originated in Burgundy, France. The Willamette Valley region in Oregon has developed a huge production of Pinot Noir due to the similar climate, soil content and style of winemaking.


Willamette Valley AVA's

The Willamette Valley as of 2020, now has a total of 9 American Viticulture Areas which include; Chehalem Mountains, Dundee Hills, Eola-Amity Hills, McMinnville, Ribbon Ridge, Yamhill-Carlton, Van Duzer Corridor, Tualatin Hills, and the Laurelwood District.



Most tasting rooms are open from 11:00am to 6:00pm, including weekends. Always check in advance if reservations are needed.
